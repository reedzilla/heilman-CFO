The Reason Why One justification per move
These build on the reputation Jack and the team have spent nearly four decades earning. None of them change what works — they carry that hard-won trust forward into how the shops price, book, and protect their work. Each move, with the one reason it earns its place.
1

Raise the labor rate — $139 → $170/hr

Nearly all margin · +$446K/yr

Joe Kranz — my old boss — just opened his own shop, Kranz Motors in Kennett Square, at $170/hr, and he's holding it, not discounting it back. He's not alone: comparable Main Line and Chester County independents have already climbed past your current rate. You've held steady for years — but four decades of earned trust have more than justified the right to match the market. Moving to $170 adds almost no cost — no extra parts, labor, or bay time — so nearly all drops to the bottom line.

Bottom lineThis isn't reaching — it's catching up to a market that's already moved past you.
2

Roll fluids into a Shop Supplies Fee — 7% of labor, capped $40

No upfront cost · +$54K/yr · automated by the system

Itemizing small consumables like washer fluid or a half-quart of oil recovers pennies, but it can rub a loyal customer the wrong way. Rolling them into a single capped shop-supplies fee recovers the same cost quietly across the ticket — and frees the team to keep giving away the small stuff that builds goodwill, the goodwill that brings the $1,500 jobs back.

Bottom lineA small structural fee protects the margin and the relationship at the same time.
3

Add a shop-management system across all 3 shops

~$1,500/mo (est.) · pays for itself by the 9th · $3.48 back per $1

The shops have run on paper and built a loyal following doing it — but across three locations, paper makes it easy to lose the last 0.2 hours of diag or cleanup, and it can't timestamp a digital authorization. A system like Tekmetric captures that work automatically, ends the phone-vs-online double-bookings that leave bays idle, and turns every authorization into a clean, PA-compliant record. Maclane Auto runs all three of its locations on Tekmetric and swears by it — a shop your size, already proving it works. The recovered time alone covers the cost in the first week and a half of each month.

Bottom linePaper got the shops this far. A system is what lets them grow without losing the details.
4

Free quick look, paid testing — diagnostics start at one hour ($170)

Free triage · real testing billed · higher close on the repair

A fast visual look stays free — that's the hook that gets a customer to leave the car, and that's half the battle. But the moment it needs real testing — a scope, a smoke test, tracing a CAN-bus fault or EVAP leak — that's a full hour of labor, $170, because it uses the most expensive tools and the most skilled hands in the building. No more giving away the answer. Pairing the testing with a video multi-point inspection — built right into Tekmetric's DVI — makes the fee easy to understand: the tech records the fault on their own phone and sends it straight to the customer, and when they see the problem on screen, they approve the repair with confidence.

Bottom lineGive away the look, never the answer. Charge for the testing — and let the video prove it's worth every penny.
